Knots

For writing that has to survive revision.

AI made prose cheap. Knots is for the work underneath: arguments, citations, structure, provenance, and revisions that need to remain navigable as they change. It treats writing as source, so humans and agents can point to exact parts of a document and revise without dissolving the work into draft churn.

View repository Read the knots memo

Early local-first system. AGPL-3.0. Built for dense, evolving intellectual work.

Source

The work becomes addressable

Sentences, paragraphs, sections, citations, and other meaningful parts of a document get stable handles. The author can point to the work instead of describing it vaguely.

History

Revision stays legible

Rewriting and rearranging are different acts. Knots records both: how content changes, and how it is placed, moved, reused, or removed inside a larger argument.

Publishing

Publication becomes compilation

Markdown, web pages, and other outputs are artifacts produced from source. The history, references, and build provenance remain inspectable upstream of the rendered surface.

Why it matters

The conversation should not become the artifact.

Current AI writing flows generate text quickly, but they often leave the author negotiating over blobs: chat transcripts, whole files, pasted drafts, and diffs that do not preserve the shape of the work.

Knots gives the work a stable internal form. The author can navigate exact source units; a model can query and revise those units; publication can happen without flattening the process into disposable draft surfaces.

ktui

Read calmly. Point precisely. Revise against source.

ktui is the interface taking shape on top of Knots: structural traversal on the left, a natural reading surface in the middle, and model composition on the right. It is not a note app with chat bolted on. It is an authoring harness where navigation, reference, and model-landed rewrites share the same source.

Current shape

What Knots provides

  • stable prefixes for semantic units
  • schema-aware document surfaces
  • derivation and composition history
  • publish refs and provenance-aware outputs
  • CLI and query paths for agents

What the interface makes possible

  • structural navigation over documents
  • focused identity reveal without metadata clutter
  • fast target selection and reversible movement
  • model changes that land back in source
